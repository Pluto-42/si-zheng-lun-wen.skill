# Course Map

Use this file to choose the right lens for different mainland China university red-course papers.

## 思想道德与法治

Best suited to topics about personal growth, values, public morality, rule of law, civic responsibility, youth responsibility, and the relation between individual life and the collective.

Good angles:

- Personal experience -> moral/legal concept -> public significance.
- Everyday public-life problem -> rule consciousness -> civic responsibility.
- Youth growth -> ideals, responsibility, discipline, and action.

Avoid:

- Turning every topic into a national strategy essay.
- Replacing personal reflection with policy vocabulary.

## 中国特色社会主义

Best suited to topics about Chinese modernization, common prosperity, socialism with Chinese characteristics, governance capacity, high-quality development, reform and opening up, institutional advantages, and contemporary policy.

Good angles:

- Real problem -> institutional logic -> policy practice -> people's livelihood.
- Development achievement -> underlying system/mechanism -> future challenge.
- Concept from textbook -> contemporary governance case.

Avoid:

- Listing policies without explaining their logic.
- Treating slogans as arguments.

## 中国近现代史纲要

Best suited to historical narrative, historical choice, road comparison, national rejuvenation, anti-imperialist and anti-feudal struggle, CPC leadership, reform, and the relation between history and reality.

Good angles:

- Historical question -> competing paths -> people's choice -> contemporary meaning.
- Event/person/group -> historical structure -> road and system conclusion.
- Past humiliation or struggle -> institutional/ideological awakening -> present responsibility.

Avoid:

- Pure chronological summary.
- Over-romanticizing historical suffering without analysis.

## 马克思主义基本原理

Best suited to concept analysis, dialectics, historical materialism, political economy, contradiction analysis, practice and cognition, capitalism/socialism, technology and society, labor, and social development laws.

Good angles:

- Concept definition -> text basis -> contradiction unfolding -> contemporary explanation.
- Phenomenon -> essence -> historical condition -> practical response.
- One-sided view -> dialectical correction -> institutional or practical implication.

Avoid:

- Quoting Marxist terms without explaining them.
- Reducing dialectics to "both sides are important."

## Cross-Course Pattern

A strong red-course essay often follows:

1. A real question or misconception.
2. A course concept that clarifies the question.
3. Historical or contemporary evidence.
4. A conclusion that returns to practice, institution, people, or youth.

If the paper feels empty, add a specific object: a law, policy, historical event, classroom concept, local example, campus observation, or personal dilemma.
