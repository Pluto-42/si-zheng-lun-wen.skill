# Mainstream Commentary Writing Patterns

These patterns distill transferable writing habits from the source index. They are not quotations. Use them to guide red-course essay structure and tone.

## Cross-Source Observations

Mainstream commentary rarely succeeds by piling up slogans. Strong pieces usually combine:

1. A concrete trigger: policy notice, public event, social phenomenon, local case, historical moment.
2. A named concept: 中国式现代化, 文化自信, 生态文明, 守正创新, 基层治理, 青年担当.
3. A causal bridge: why the concept explains the trigger.
4. A practice path: system, policy, social participation, youth action, or historical lesson.
5. A measured conclusion: return to people, practice, institution, or responsibility.

## Source-Specific Patterns

### 人民网 / 人民时评

Useful for: balanced, compact, policy-aware red-course essays.

Typical pattern:

1. Begin with a current event, policy, or visible social scene.
2. Introduce the core concept in plain terms.
3. Explain the mechanism, not only the attitude.
4. Use two or three concrete examples.
5. End with expectation or action, not excessive lyricism.

Transfer to student paper:

- Use one policy or social observation as the entry point.
- Keep paragraphs short and directional.
- Make one sentence in each paragraph answer "why".

Risk:

- Easy to imitate as official-sounding prose without student thinking. Add course concepts or personal observation.

### 求是网

Useful for: theory-heavy paragraphs, 中国特色社会主义, 马克思主义基本原理, official concept definitions.

Typical pattern:

1. Start from major theory, speech, or historical mission.
2. Define a concept through its historical and theoretical position.
3. Use broad historical span: past exploration, present practice, future mission.
4. Emphasize relationships: theory/practice, inheritance/innovation, development/protection.
5. Close with direction and principle.

Transfer to student paper:

- Use 求是-style material for conceptual accuracy, not for every paragraph.
- Pair each theoretical statement with a concrete example.
- For 马原 papers, convert big concepts into contradiction analysis.

Risk:

- Too much 求是-style phrasing can make a student paper sound detached. Balance with concrete course discussion.

### 光明网 / 光明日报

Useful for: culture, education, ecology, red resources, scholarly but readable commentary.

Typical pattern:

1. Name a recent document, educational activity, or cultural phenomenon.
2. Bring in a scholar or discipline-aware viewpoint.
3. Explain value through culture, education, or civilization language.
4. Offer a practice path with several dimensions.
5. Use moderate literary color.

Transfer to student paper:

- Good for paragraphs that need "思想深度 + 文气".
- Useful for culture and ecology papers where the conclusion should feel warmer than a policy memo.

Risk:

- Some expressions are highly polished; simplify if they obscure the argument.

### 中青在线 / 中国青年报

Useful for: youth-themed red-course papers and student-perspective writing.

Typical pattern:

1. Begin with how youth encounter a national theme.
2. Translate abstract theory into study, work, creativity, service, or digital life.
3. Recognize youth's real pressures and needs.
4. Connect personal growth with national development.
5. End with action and growth, not only emotional uplift.

Transfer to student paper:

- Add campus, volunteer, internship, reading, social media, or career examples.
- Use "小我/大我" carefully; explain the bridge rather than just naming it.

Risk:

- Youth-language can become motivational. Keep one concrete practice scene.

### 半月谈

Useful for: problem-conscious governance analysis and anti-formalism paragraphs.

Typical pattern:

1. Name the problem sharply.
2. Show lived consequences.
3. Diagnose institutional causes.
4. Distinguish individual style problems from system problems.
5. Propose reform through rights/responsibilities, incentives, resources, and procedures.

Transfer to student paper:

- Excellent for "基层治理", "形式主义", "制度优势", "国家治理现代化".
- Use when the paper needs realism and specificity.

Risk:

- Tone can be sharp; for classroom essays, keep criticism within constructive institutional analysis.

## Reusable Red-Course Structures

### Structure A: Concrete Scene To Theory

Use for 思想道德与法治 and youth papers.

1. A campus/social scene.
2. The value conflict behind the scene.
3. Course concept that clarifies the conflict.
4. Action path for students.

Example skeleton:

在[具体场景]中，问题表面上是[现象]，实质上关涉[价值/制度/责任]。用[课程概念]来看，它提醒我们不能只停留在情绪判断，而要看到[深层关系]。因此，青年学生的回应不应只是表态，而应落实到[具体行动]。

### Structure B: Historical Road Choice

Use for 中国近现代史纲要.

1. Historical dilemma.
2. Failed or limited attempts.
3. CPC-led path and people's choice.
4. Present confidence and responsibility.

Example skeleton:

近代中国面对的不是单一困难，而是民族危机、制度危机和文化危机交织的历史困局。多种方案先后登场又相继受挫，说明道路选择不能离开中国具体实际。正是在这种历史比较中，[中心论点]才获得了实践根据。

### Structure C: Dialectical Relation

Use for 马克思主义基本原理 and ecology/culture papers.

1. Identify a pair of apparent opposites.
2. Explain why they are not isolated.
3. Show condition and boundary.
4. Apply to current practice.

Good relation pairs:

- 传承与创新
- 发展与保护
- 活力与秩序
- 效率与公平
- 个人理想与社会需要
- 理论自信与问题意识

Example skeleton:

[A]与[B]并不是互相排斥的两端。离开[A]，[B]容易失去根基；缺少[B]，[A]又可能停留在旧有形态。关键在于通过[制度/实践/方法]把二者统一到[目标]之中。

### Structure D: Problem To Governance

Use for 中国特色社会主义, 形式主义, 基层治理, 法治.

1. Concrete problem.
2. Harm to people/work/order.
3. Institutional cause.
4. Reform path.
5. Link to governance modernization.

Example skeleton:

如果只把[问题]理解为少数人的作风偏差，就会低估其治理含义。更深层看，它暴露出[权责/激励/程序/资源]上的不匹配。解决这一问题，需要从[制度路径]入手，把治理重心从临时整治转向常态机制。
